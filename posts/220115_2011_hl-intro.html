My intro for the Hive Learners community.

This is my introduction post for joining the HIVE Learners community, as instructed in this post. It includes a photograph of myself holding a piece of paper with my HIVE username, today's date, and the name of the country that I live in. It also includes the requisite "three or four more photos". It is 470 words long, excluding this summary and the image credits section at the bottom.

Who I am

Tēnā koutou, tēnā koutou, tēnā koutou katoa. I bring you all greetings in the indigenous language of the Māori of Aotearoa / New Zealand, where I live.

My username is @frittro, and that is my preferred name. I am a 50+ year old "elder geek". I'm married to @frittni for over 21 years now. We do not have any children, unfortunately. We live in West Auckland, a suburb of Aotearoa / New Zealand's largest city, (but it is not the capitol, which is in Wellington). Both my wife and I live with the effects of disability – she was born with Spina Bifida; and I have various issues including autism, type 2 diabetes, depression, and a cardiac condition, with stents in my heart.

I am a new Hivean, but I used to be on the Steem blockchain back in 2018 or so, under the username of @trisquelwhare.

Why I am here now

I came back to the social blockchain this year to blog about my new project, which involves #selfhosting at home on a #raspberrypi single board computer. When I learned about the intervening history of the Steem blockchain, and the mass migration of the userbase here to HIVE, I decided to tag along here, as well. There is a lot for me to re-learn about being on a social blockchain, along with the new features of HIVE itself. I've joined three learning communties (they didn't even have such a thing as "communities" when I was last on Steem), including Silver Bloggers, The Terminal, and this one, Hive Learners. I want to learn as much as I can about blogging here on HIVE, as quickly as I can, so that I can concentrate on my new project, which is what I'm really here for.
