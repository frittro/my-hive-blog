On mental health.

Today we came down to the park here to meet up with a few friends from Te Ata, which is a mental health peer-support group that I belong to. This is the first time that we had met together in-person this year, and for quite some time before that, due to the COVID-19 lockdowns. It was good to catch up with them again, but sad to hear that one of the staff is planning to leave, this year.

In 2010, I was diagnosed with a major depressive disorder, and it was during this period that I came to be diagnosed also as being on the Autism Spectrum. In fact, that was also the same year that I was diagnosed with type 2 diabetes! It all hit me at once. No wonder I'm so screwed up, haha!

Te Ata has been really helpful to me though, giving me something that I can look forward to during the week. It is a pity that the pandemic has spoiled that a bit, recently. Hopefully we can keep this new Omicron strain out of New Zealand, so that activities like Te Ata can resume again.

Anyway, this was just a brief update. I'm still working on my homelab project, and I will be blogging again about that soon.
